GeoQuest

The closest you can get to geocaching in a box!

INTRODUCTION and GOAL

Geocaching is a high-tech adventure game enjoyed world-wide by players equipped with GPS receivers. The basic idea is to find containers called geocaches, hidden outdoors by other players. The geocaches are often located in interesting places that you might not otherwise find. If you're not familiar with geocaching, you can learn about it at www.geocaching.com.

GeoQuest is a board game based on geocaching, for 2 to 6 players, ages 12 and up. Once you learn how to play, a typical games takes about 30 minutes. The object of the game is to be the first player to reach 25 points, or find all of the caches on the board. You gain points by finding caches, helping Travel Bugs reach their goal, or from various events that happen randomly during the game. It is possible to lose points, but you can never go below zero.

If you're just learning to play, you can skip the sections highlighted like this for now.

CONTENTS

Your GeoQuest game comes with the following components:
  • Game Board (includes map frame and 4 double-sided map sections)
  • Two special 6-sided dice (for moving, searching, and triggering events)
  • A 30-sided Alphabet die (for rolling random locations)
  • Colored Cacher tokens (1 per player, for marking your position on the map)
  • Colored chips (1 set per player, for marking caches as they are found)
  • Three decks of cards: Cacher Characters, Equipment, and Events
  • Cache disks showing the type and difficulty rating of the caches (placed on the map board to mark cache locations)
  • Puzzle Category chips (used for Puzzle caches; see below)
  • Six color-coded Cacher Tracking panels
  • Assorted Point and First-to-Find (FTF) coins, for keeping track of your points
  • Travel Bug disks (these show the Travel Bug's goal)
  • A few blank cards are provided for you to make up your own.

THE MAP BOARD

The map board is made up of separate sections called quadrants. Grid lines are marked so that each section is divided into 169 (13 x 13) smaller squares called tiles. Four sections are placed together in the map frame to create the map board. Each section is double-sided; you may place either side face up. The sections are square; you may rotate them any way you like. Sometimes paths or streams from one section may connect with another, but it's okay if they don't. By flipping, moving, or rotating the sections, you can produce thousands of different map boards.

The five types of terrain on the map board, along with paths and streams.
Urban Clear Forest Swamp Rocky Path Stream

There are five types of terrain on the map board, as well as Paths and Streams. Even though some Rocky tiles might have a few trees, or some Forest tiles might have clear spots, each tile is treated as a single terrain type. Tiles can contain Paths or Streams, but these do not affect the terrain type.

The type of terrain does not affect movement directly. However, some types of equipment let you move faster on certain types of terrain. Similarly, some events can have different effects depending on what type of terrain you are in when they occur. Paths and Streams do affect movement (see the MOVEMENT section below).

Special Locations

There are several special locations marked on the map. Some of these are man-made, such as the Zoo, the Cemetery, the Hospital, and the Police Station. Others are natural, such as the Waterfalls and the Scenic Views. These locations are used by some of the Event cards, and also by some of the Travel Bugs.

PREPARATION

Unfold the map frame, and arrange the 4 map quadrants to form the map board.

INITIAL CACHE PLACEMENT

  1. Pick 8 random Cache Disks, and place them face-up on any map tiles (except the lake). Only one cache can occupy a tile. Spread them around the board (2 in each quadrant), among the various terrain types. For any Puzzle caches, select a random Puzzle Category chip, and place it under the cache chip.
  2. Add a FTF coin under each cache disk; these are bonus prizes (worth 1 point) for the first person to find the cache.
  3. Each player draws one Travel Bug, and places that chip under any cache chip.

Any player may view any of these tokens at any time; the puzzle categories or Travel Bugs are not secret. Usually it's best to keep the Cache Disk on top, since that's the most important one to know about when you're planning your moves.

PLAYER SETUP

Shuffle the Equipment and Event cards (separately), and place the decks within reach. Give each player the following:

  • one of the colored Cacher Tokens (to mark your position on the map)
  • the matching set of colored chips (to mark caches you've found)
  • the matching Tracking Panel (to help organize your items)
  • one random Cacher Character card
  • one random Equipment card
  • one random Travel Bug chip

Note that Cacher Characters also have unique advantages and disadvantages. For example, Athletic Amanda gets +1 when searching in Rocky terrain. Don't forget to apply these special rules during the game. Each player starts with a random Equipment card. Place that card on your Tracking Panel.

STARTING LOCATION

After all caches have been placed, roll the Alphabet die to determine your starting location. Each Cacher Token starts on one of the Latitude/Longitude Arrows around the map frame. There are 4 arrows for each letter (one on each side of the map); you may start on any of these four arrows not already occupied by another player's token. You can use one of your colored chips to mark your starting location; an Event may require you to return to that spot later.

PLAYING THE GAME

TURN SEQUENCE

A turn refers to a single player. A round refers to one turn for all players (it still counts as a round, even if some players had to skip their turn).

Each turn has two parts: (1) events, and (2) moving/searching. At the beginning of your turn, you must resolve any Event card which has been played on you. Follow the instructions on the Event card. In some cases, this may cause you to end your turn before you get a chance to roll.

Each turn, you declare whether you are going to Move, or Search. You may only Search if your token is located in the same tile as a Cache you have not yet found. You roll both 6-sided dice to Move or Search and add the numbers together to determine how far you can move (or how well you searched).

The dice are slightly different. Both have sides with pips like normal dice, representing numbers from zero to four. Various combinations are possible, so that you can roll a total between 0 and 7; the average is 3. Rolling doubles triggers an event; see the EVENTS section below for more information. Each die also has one special (red or green) side with a cache icon, which is explained in the SEARCHING and New Cache Event sections below.

MOVING

Normal Movement
When moving, you may move your token up to the total number you rolled on both dice (plus any bonuses you may get). This can be from 0 to 7 tiles (or more with bonuses). You don't have to move the full amount. Each step of your move must be to an adjacent tile; you may not move diagonally. The FIND and DNF sides count as zero.
Paths

Using a Path gives you a +1 bonus when moving. Each turn, your first move along a Path is free. Even if you roll zero, if your token is on a Path, you can move one tile along that path. Note that all Urban tiles count as Paths. You don't get the free move just by crossing a path.

Streams
Unlike Paths, Streams occur at the edge between tiles. Crossing a stream costs 3 movement points, so you'll need to roll at least a 3 (including any move bonuses) in order to get across. If you roll less than 3, you can still move, but you can't cross the stream. This rule does not apply if you cross the Stream using one of the bridges.

Movement Bonuses

Some Cacher Characters and Equipment cards gives you a bonus (or penalty) to move. For example, if you have the Binoculars, you may move an extra tile if you rolled a 4 or higher. In some cases, this bonus requires a certain type of terrain. For example, the Hiking Boots give you a +1 when you are moving in Forest terrain (you get the +1 if you move into any Forest tile during your turn). You may use more than one movement bonus per turn. So, if you happen to have the Hiking Boots and the Binoculars, you can combine them to move 2 extra tiles on a single turn.

Jumping

Some equipment (such as the FRS Radio) and events (such as Broken Wrist and Meet and Greet) allow you to jump to other locations. This simply means that you move your token to another location, ignoring any movement bonuses, penalties, or other terrain effects (such as Stream crossing restrictions).

SEARCHING

Each cache has a Difficulty Rating -- from 1 (the easiest) to 5 (the hardest). In order to find the cache, you must roll equal to or higher than the Difficulty Rating. Some Cacher Characters and Equipment cards gives you a bonus (or penalty) to search. For example, if you have the Flashlight, you add 1 to your roll if you're searching for a cache no one has found yet.

One die has a special FIND side. If you roll this, you automatically find any cache you are searching for. The other die has a special DNF (did not find) side. If you roll the DNF, you fail to find the cache (no matter what your rolled on the other die). You must wait until your next turn to try again. Otherwise, apply any bonuses or penalties to determine your Search score. If your Search score is equal to or higher than the cache Difficulty Rating, you find the cache!

Geocachers often team up to find caches. Any time two or more cachers are both on the same tile, they all get credit for the find if any one of them finds the cache. Only the player who makes the successful Search roll gets the FTF bonus point. If another play finds the cache for you, you get the points and equipment on your next turn; you don't need to roll (and you don't get to move).

FINDING A CACHE

When you find a cache:
  • get a Point Coin equal to the Difficulty Rating of the cache
  • if you were the first to find this cache, take the FTF coin
  • put one of your colored chips under the cache chip to show you found it
  • draw two Equipment cards, and put one Equipment card back in the deck
  • take and/or leave Travel Bugs (see the Travel Bug section)
Finding a cache is the main way to earn points and get equipment in the game. You score points equal to the cache Difficulty Rating (add the appropriate coin to your pile of Point coins on your Tracking Panel). If you were the final player to find a cache (in other words, all players have now found it), you remove it from the board. Otherwise, put one of your colored chips under the cache chip. This allows everyone to keep track of which players have found which caches.

When you find a cache while geocaching, you typically trade items. This means you take a trinket out of the cache, and leave something to replace it. You should leave something of equal or greater value. When you find a cache in the GeoQuest game, you get to draw 2 Equipment cards from the deck. You can keep either (or both) of these, but you must return 1 Equipment card (to the bottom of the deck). So, you'll gain 1 Equipment card each time you find a cache (as long as you have room for the Equipment).

GEOCACHES

Normal (Traditional) Caches

Caches are represented by the Cache Chip on the map. Each cache has a type which corresponds to the picture on the chip. Each cache also has a Difficulty Rating (from 1 to 5), which is shown in a circle on the bottom-right side of the chip. For example, the cache on the left is a Normal one, and has a Difficulty rating of 2. Normal caches have no special rules associated with them; the other cache types are described below.

Multi Caches

Multiple-stage caches (Multi cache for short) require you to find one or more stages, before you can find the actual cache. In geocaching, each stage usually contains a clue or the coordinates that lead to the next stage. In GeoQuest, you must make a separate Search roll for each stage. The number of stages is specified on the cache chip. To find a 2-stage Multi cache (with Difficulty 3) like the one shown, you would have to make 2 successful Search rolls of 3 or better.

Each time you successfully find a stage, you may continue rolling to try and find the next stage. So it is possible to find a Multi cache in a single turn, although it's unlikely (especially for those level 4 or higher). You only have to find each stage once (keep count each time you make a successful Search roll); you don't have to start over each turn. If more than one cacher is on the same spot, each successful Search roll counts for everyone on the team. Note that only the first roll on any turn may trigger an event.

Puzzle Caches

Puzzle caches require that you solve a puzzle in order to get the location of the cache. In GeoQuest, the row and column letters (of the tile where the cache is hidden) are used as part of the puzzle. Before a player can search for the cache, she must solve the puzzle by supplying a name that contains those two letters and fits the Category (shown on the puzzle category chip). The letters can appear anywhere in the solution, in any order. The puzzle can only be solved when the player's token is at the cache location.

Let's say you want to find a Puzzle cache with the category of Animals at row U and column Z. You could solve this puzzle using the word "buzzard". Another player could also solve it using a different animal such as "zebra mussel". Multi-word names are acceptable; some categories allow proper names as well.

Each player who wants to search for the cache must come up with her own solution. Once you've solved the puzzle, treat it just like a normal cache. You still need to make a successful Search roll in order to find it. If you fail to find it with your first Search roll, that's okay. You don't need to solve the puzzle again; just continue to search as you would for any other cache.

EQUIPMENT

Various types of geocaching gear are represented in the game using the deck of Equipment cards. Although you don't need any special equipment to geocache (other than a GPS receiver), some of it can be quite helpful. Sometimes the equipment will have a built-in bonus, such as the Flashlight, which helps you when searching.

Other Equipment cards can prevent the effects of certain events. For example, the Insect Repellent can prevent the Mosquitoes or Ticks from bothering you. Most of the time you just need to have the Equipment card on your Tracking Panel to benefit from it. Sometimes, you must discard the card in order to use it. For example, the First-aid Kit can be discarded to prevent various injury events in the game. Unlike Event cards, when an Equipment card is discarded, shuffle it back into the deck.

You may have up to 4 Equipment cards (played face up on your Tracking Panel). If you already have all the Equipment cards that you are allowed when you draw a new one, you must either discard it or one of your other Equipment cards. Some Equipment cards (such as the Backpack) allow you to have more than 4 Equipment cards in play.

EQUIPMENT COMBOS

At the bottom of each Equipment card, there is a Combo name. For example, the Binoculars and Field Guide cards comprise the Bird Watcher Combo. Each Combo is made up of two Equipment cards. If you're lucky enough to get both cards in a particular Combo, you get a special bonus (shown below). You can refer to the list below to remind yourself of the effects.

Some of these special effects (such as the Bird Watcher Combo) are triggered when a particular roll is made. Only rolls that count have a chance of triggering Combo bonuses. When a game effect (such as Determined Dan) allows a player to re-roll the dice, the first roll is ignored. However if a player takes another turn, each of those rolls count, and therefore each roll has a chance of triggering a Combo bonus. When a player makes multiple rolls while searching for a Multi cache, only the first roll has a chance to trigger events or combo effects.

EVENTS

Whenever you want to Move or Search, you roll the two 6-sided dice. If you roll doubles, you draw an Event card (before moving or searching). Don't reveal the card to the other players yet. You may keep it, or play it on yourself or any other player. Players may only keep one Event card at a time though, so if you already have one, you'll have to play or discard one of them.

To play an Event card, simply place it face-up in front of the player you want to target. The event will take effect at the beginning of that player's next turn. Only one Event card can be played on a person at a time though, so you can't target a player who already has an Event card in play. Unlike Equipment cards, when an Event card is discarded or resolved, remove it from the game.

Special Events

Most events only affect the current player. In some cases, an event may affect more that one player, or may last one or more rounds. See the Clarifications section if you have questions about these events. Some event cards (such as Fresh Snow) are marked Resolve Now. If you draw one of these, you must follow the instructions immediately, before moving or searching. Others (such as Helpful Hint) are marked Discard To Resolve. You may discard this type of event card (after you roll, if you like), to have it take effect.

New Cache Event and Random Cache Placement

If you roll both Cache icons (the FIND and DNF sides), you have triggered a New Cache Event. Your turn is over (you don't move, or find any cache you may have been searching for), but you get to add a new cache onto the board, and you earn 2 points! Randomly select a cache chip to place.

New caches should be placed randomly. You can choose a random location by rolling the Alphabet die twice (once to determine the latitude/row, and once to determine the longitude/column). The rows and columns are marked with the letters of the alphabet. For example, if you roll "S" and "V", place the cache token in row S, column V. Two caches cannot be on the same tile; roll again if necessary to avoid that.

The Alphabet die has all 26 letters of the alphabet, as well as 4 wilds. If you roll one of the wilds, you can choose any letter you like. If you roll a wild for your column (for example), you may choose any tile along the row you rolled. If you roll wild on your first roll, you may roll for the column before choosing which row you want.

TRAVEL BUGS

Travel Bugs are represented in GeoQuest using small chips. Players start the game with one Travel Bug, and can acquire more by finding a cache that contains them. If the cache you found contains any Travel Bugs, you may take one. If you do, move it from the map onto your Tracking Panel. You may only take one Travel Bug, and only if you don't already have two.

If you have any Travel Bugs with you when you end your turn on a tile that contains a cache that you have found, you may leave them in that cache. To do so, move the Travel Bug chip from your Tracking Panel onto the cache. You can leave any number of these in the cache; you gain 1 point for each one you leave. You can't leave one in the same cache that you got it from.

Each Travel Bug has a goal (shown on the Travel Bug chip). If you help it reach its goal, then you score 3 points (instead of the 1 point you normally get). For example, you could leave the Travel Bug shown on the right in any cache which is next to a Stream to achieve its goal. You may want to re-visit a cache which you have previously found in order to leave a Travel Bug. That's fine; you don't have to search for the cache again once you've found it; just end your turn on the tile containing that cache.

Sometimes a Travel Bug might start out in a cache that would satisfy its goal; if that happens, you must find another cache in order to earn the 3 points. When a Travel Bug reaches its goal, it is removed from the game. In some cases, it may not be possible for a Travel Bug to reach its goal; but you can still score 1 point by leaving it in a cache.

CLARIFICATIONS

Unless the card says otherwise... Some cards will contradict the basic game rules. When that happens, follow the instructions on the card.

Cache Characters

  • Jolly Jamie: The Geocoin Equipment cards do count towards a player's total points, but the CITO Bag does not.
  • Independent Isabel: A map quadrant is the separate board piece that represents a quarter of the total map area.

Puzzle Cache Categories

  • 5-LETTER WORDS: The solution must be a five letter word.
  • ___ & ___ PHRASES: The solution must be a common phrase such as Salt & Pepper (don't count the word "and").
  • BLUE or YELLOW: The solution must be something that is either blue or yellow (or both).
  • FIRST & LAST: The solution can be any word, with the puzzle letters as the first and last letters in the word (either order).
  • SIDE BY SIDE: The solution can be any word, but the puzzle letters must be next to each other.

Equipment and Combos

  • Cell Phone: You may choose to take the +1 bonus after you roll.
  • First-aid Kit: Injury Event cards include Blisters, Broken Wrist, and Twisted Ankle.
  • Insulation: For example, if the Litterbug event card is played on you, you would only lose 1 point instead of 4.
  • Karma: If the event is discarded, other events may still be played on you this turn.
  • Naturalist: Wildlife Event cards include 13-Year Cicadas, Bear Encounter, Fire Ants, Fox Hunt, Ivory-billed Woodpecker, Killer Bees, Mosquitoes, Ticks, and What Kind of Snake Is That?.
  • Weatherman: Weather Event cards include Bad Weather, Caught in the Rain, Winter is Coming, Drought, Early Frost, Flash Flood, Fresh Snow, and Heavy Rain.

Events

  • 13-Year Cicadas: This event lasts two rounds. In other words, it affects all players for their next two turns. Place this card on the map board, so all players can see it. Remove it from the game after it has been in effect for two rounds. Although only one Event card may be played per turn, other events may be played while a multi-turn event is in effect. Other Event cards that last a round or more include: Drought, Fresh Snow, Heavy Rain, and Solar Flares.
  • Bug Swap: If no one else has a Travel Bug, ignore this event.
  • Cache Pirates: Any Travel Bug in the cache is removed from the game.
  • Equipment Rental: If you lose the equipment you rented for any reason, return it to the deck.
  • Eureka!: If you're having trouble solving a particular Puzzle cache, you may play this card instead. Or you can play it to find a hard cache. It only replaces as a single Search roll though, so you would still have to roll for the other stages of a Multi cache. Since this is a Discard To Resolve card, you may play it after you roll.
  • Meet and Greet: This is known as an Event Cache in geocaching. Roll the Alphabet die twice to determine the location of the party.
  • Lost and Found: If the player you give the card to doesn't have room, that player must discard a card (they may discard the one you gave them).
  • May I Borrow That?: You can only borrow a pack Equipment card (such as the Backpack, Belt Pack, Hydration Pack, Safari Vest, Swag Bag or Waterproof Jacket) if doing so would not cause that player to have more Equipment cards than he's allowed to hold. You can't borrow rented equipment.
  • Shortcut: Allows you to move up to 6 tiles even if you cross a stream.
  • Upgrade: If there is more than one player with the fewest Equipment cards, you may choose the player. See the Lost and Found clarification above.
