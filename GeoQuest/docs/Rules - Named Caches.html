GPS QUEST

The closest you can get to geocaching in a box!

GOAL

The object of the game is to be the first player to reach 25 points, or find all of the caches on the board. You gain points by finding caches, helping travel bugs reach their goal, or from various events that happen randomly during the game. It is also possible to lose points, but you can never go below zero.

If you're just learning to play, you can skip the sections highlighted like this for now.

CONTENTS

Your GPS Quest game comes with the following components:
  • Game Board (includes map frame, 4 map sections, and the Active Caches Panel)
  • Two special 6-sided dice (for moving, searching, and triggering events)
  • A 30-sided Alphabet die (for rolling random locations)
  • Colored Cacher tokens (1 per player, for marking your position on the map)
  • Colored pegs (1 set per player, for marking caches as they are found)
  • Three decks of cards (Cacher Characters, Equipment, and Events)
  • Numbered Cache boxes (placed on the map board to mark cache locations)
  • Six color-coded Cacher Tracking panels
  • Assorted Point and FTF coins (for keeping track of your points)
  • Cache Labels (these show the name, type and difficulty rating of the caches)
  • Travel Bug labels (these show the Travel Bug's goal)

A few blank cards are provided for each deck, as well as some blank cache labels and travel bugs. These aren't required in the game; they are for you to use so you can make up your own Cacher Characters, Equipment, Events, Geocaches, Travel Bugs, and Combos. If you do invent some fun ones, be sure to post them at our web site (www.gpsquest.com), so other players can share them!

THE MAP BOARD

The map board is made up of separate sections, generally referred to as quadrants. Grid lines are marked so that each section is divided into 169 (13 x 13) smaller squares called tiles. Four sections are placed together in the map frame to create the map board. Each section is double-sided; you may place either side face up. The sections are square; you may rotate them any way you like. Sometimes paths or streams from one section may connect with another, but it's okay if they don't. By flipping, moving, or rotating the sections, you can produce thousands of different map boards.

Terrain

The five types of terrain on the map board, as well as paths and streams.
Urban Clear Forest Swamp Rocky Path Stream

There are five types of terrain on the map board, as well as Paths and Streams. Even though some Rocky tiles might have a few trees, or some Forest tiles might have clear spots, each tile is treated as a single terrain type. Tiles can contain Paths or Streams, but these do not affect the terrain type.

The type of terrain does not affect movement directly. However, some types of equipment let you move faster on certain types of terrain. Similarly, some events can have different effects depending on what type of terrain you are in when they occur. Paths and Streams do affect movement (see the MOVEMENT section below).

Special Locations

There are several special locations marked on the map. Some of these are man-made, such as the Zoo, the Cemetery, the Hospital, and the Police Station. Others are natural, such as the Waterfalls and the Scenic Views. These locations are used by some of the Event cards, and also by some of the Travel Bugs.

PREPARATION

Assemble the map frame, and arrange the 4 map quadrants to form the map board.

PLAYER SETUP

Cacher Tracking Panel

Shuffle the Event cards, and place them within reach. Give each player the following:

  • one of the colored Cacher Tokens
  • the matching set of colored pegs
  • the matching Tracking Panel
  • one random Cacher Character card
  • one random Travel Bug
  • one random Event card

The Tracking Panel is used to keep track of your Cacher Character, Points coins, Equipment cards, and Travel Bugs during the game. The Cacher token is used to mark your position on the map board. The colored pegs are used on the Active Caches Panel (on the right side of the map) to mark the caches that you have found. You may look at your Event card at any time, but don't show it to the other players yet.

Each Cacher Character starts with a particular item (usually an Equipment card). Find that card, and place it on your Tracking Panel. For example, if you drew Birder Brandon, you start with the Binoculars Equipment card. Most Cacher Characters also have unique advantages and disadvantages. For example, Collector Colin may carry any number of Travel Bugs. Don't forget to apply these special rules during the game. Once everyone has found his or her starting equipment, you may shuffle the remaining Equipment cards, and place the deck nearby.

STARTING LOCATION

After all caches have been placed, roll the Alphabet die to determine your starting location. Each Cacher Token starts on one of the Latitude/Longitude Arrows around the map frame. There are 4 arrows for each letter (one on each side of the map); you may start on any of these four arrows not already occupied by another player's token.

PLAYING THE GAME

TURN SEQUENCE

A turn refers to a single player. A round refers to one turn for all players (it still counts as a round, even if some players had to skip their turn).

Each turn has two parts: (1) events, and (2) moving/searching. At the beginning of your turn, you may choose to play an Event card. If you do not, then another player may play on Event card on you -- the option passes to the player whose turn is next. Only one event may be played per turn. If an event is played, follow the instructions on the Event card. In some cases, this may cause you to end your turn before you get a chance to roll.

Each turn, you declare whether you are going to Move, or Search. You may only Search if your token is located in the same tile as a Cache you have not yet found. You roll both 6-sided dice to Move or Search and add the numbers together to determine how far you can move (or how well you searched).

TBD-pictures
The dice are different. Both have sides with pips like normal dice, representing the numbers 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4. Various combinations are possible, so that you can roll a total between 0 and 7; the average is 3. The color of each numbered side is either yellow or blue. The colors matter if you roll doubles, or for certain bonuses. Rolling doubles triggers an event; see the EVENTS section below for more information. Each die also has one special (red or green) side with a cache icon, which is discussed in the SEARCHING and New Cache Event sections below.

MOVING

Normal Movement
When moving, you may move your token up to the total number you rolled on both dice (plus any bonuses you may get). This can be from 0 to 7 tiles (or more with bonuses). You don't have to move the full amount. Each step of your move must be to an adjacent tile; you may not move diagonally. The FIND and DNF sides count as zero.
Paths
If you end your turn on a tile containing a Path, you may move one extra tile along that Path. This can be a significant advantage, so you should try to stay on a Path as long as possible before heading to the cache. All Urban tiles count as Paths.
Streams
Unlike Paths, Streams occur at the edge between tiles. Streams may be crossed, but they must be crossed carefully. You may only cross a Stream if your Move roll (not counting any bonuses) is 3 or less. If you roll 4 or higher, you can still move your token if you like, but you can't cross the Stream. Whenever you cross a Stream, you must end your turn, even if you have extra moves remaining. These rules do not apply if you cross the Stream using one of the bridges.

Movement Bonuses

Some Cacher Characters and Equipment cards gives you a bonus (or penalty) to move. For example, if you have the Binoculars, you may move an extra tile if you rolled a 4 or higher. In some cases, this bonus requires a certain type of terrain. These bonuses work the same way the Path bonus does. For example, the Hiking Boots give you a +1 when you are moving in Forest terrain. If you finish your move on that type of terrain, then you may move one additional tile as long as you will still be in a Forest tile.

You still get to use your bonuses even if you roll a zero. So, if you're on a tile with a Path, you can move at least one tile (if you stay on the Path). You may use more than one movement bonus per turn. So, if you happen to have the Hiking Boots and the Binoculars, you can combine them to get +2 on a single turn. This includes the Path bonus too; so you would get +2 when using the Hiking Boots on a Forest tile with a Path.

Jumping

Some equipment (such as the FRS Radio) and events (such as Broken Wrist and Meet and Greet) allow you to jump to other locations. This simply means that you move your token to another location, ignoring any movement bonuses, penalties, or other terrain effects (such as Stream crossing restrictions).

SEARCHING

Each cache has a Difficulty Rating -- from 1 (the easiest) to 5 (the hardest). In order to find the cache, you must roll equal to or higher than the Difficulty Rating. Some Cacher Characters and Equipment cards gives you a bonus (or penalty) to search. For example, if you have the Flashlight, you add 1 to your Search roll when searching for any Micro cache.

One die has a special FIND side. If you roll this, you automatically find any cache you are searching for. The other die has a special DNF (did not find) side. If you roll the DNF, you fail to find the cache (no matter what your rolled on the other die). You must wait until your next turn to try again. Otherwise, apply any bonuses or penalties to determine your Search score. If your Search score is equal to or higher than the cache Difficulty Rating, you find the cache!

Geocachers often team up to find caches. Any time two or more cachers are both on the same tile, they all get credit for the find if any one of them finds the cache. Only the player who makes the successful Search roll gets the FTF bonus point, and draws more than one Equipment card (see the First To Find section). The other players don't roll on their turn, they just collect their Point coins and Equipment card.

FINDING A CACHE

When you find a cache:
  • get a Point Coin equal to the Difficulty Rating of the cache
  • put one of your colored pegs into the Active Caches Panel to show you found this cache
  • draw an Equipment card
  • take and/or leave Travel Bugs (see the Travel Bug section)
Finding a cache is the main way to earn points in the game. You score points equal to the cache Difficulty Rating (add the appropriate coin to your pile of Point coins on your Tracking Panel). If you were the last player to find a cache (in other words, all players have now found it), you remove it from the board, and from the Active Caches Panel. This makes room for another cache that might be added later. Otherwise, put one of your colored pegs into the proper cache slot on the Active Caches Panel. This allows everyone to keep track of which players have found which caches.

First To Find (FTF)

If you were the first player to find the cache, you get a bonus. Take the FTF coin from the appropriate slot on the Active Caches Panel, and add it to your pile of point coins; it's worth 1 point. Instead of drawing a single Equipment card, you may get to draw multiple cards (equal to the cache Difficulty Rating). Look at them and choose the one you want to keep, and shuffle the others back into the deck. For example, if you are the first to find a cache with Difficulty Rating of 3, you would draw three Equipment cards, and choose the one you want to keep.

GEOCACHES

Specific caches are represented by the Cache Labels on the Active Caches Panel, and by a Cache Box token on the map. Each cache has a name (most are named after actual geocaches) , a type (Traditional, Micro, Multi, or Puzzle), and a Difficulty Rating (from 1 to 5). A few are hybrids, such as a multi-stage puzzle cache. Some caches have special rules that are noted on the Cache Label, and may be described in more detail in the Clarifications section below.

MICRO CACHES

Micro caches are just like normal caches, except they are too small to contain items for trading. So, when you find a Micro cache, you don't get to draw any Equipment cards, and you can't leave any Travel Bugs in the cache.

MULTI-CACHES

Multiple-stage caches (multi-cache for short) require you to find one or more stages, before you can find the actual cache. In geocaching, each stage usually contains a clue or the coordinates that lead to the next stage. These caches are typically more challenging than normal single-stage caches. In GPS Quest, you must make a separate Search roll for each stage. The number of stages is specified on the cache label. For example, Tube Torcher is a famous 3-stage multi-cache with a Difficulty Rating of 5. To find it, you would have to make 3 successful Search rolls.

Each time you successfully find a stage, you may continue rolling to try and find the next stage. So it is possible to find a multi-cache in a single turn, although it's unlikely (especially for those level 4 or higher). You only have to find each stage once (keep count each time you make a successful Search roll); you don't have to start over each turn. If more than one cacher is on the same spot, each successful Search roll counts for everyone on the team. Note that each roll you make may trigger an event, but only one event can be played per turn (so you may end up ignoring some triggers).

PUZZLE CACHES

Unlike Traditional caches, puzzle caches require that you solve a puzzle in order to get the coordinates to the cache. In GPS Quest, we use the row and column letters (of the tile where the cache is hidden) as part of the puzzle. Before a player can Search for the cache, he or she must solve the puzzle by supplying a name that contains those two letters and fits the Category (listed on the puzzle cache label). The letters can appear anywhere in the solution, in any order. The puzzle can only be solved when the player's token is at the cache location.

For example, let's say you have placed the Primal Instinct cache at row U and column Z. The category of this puzzle cache is Animals. You could solve this puzzle using the word "buzzard". Another player could also solve it using a different animal such as "zebra mussel" (multi-word names are acceptable). Some categories allow proper names as well. If you have access to field guides or other sources of names, we encourage you to use them. That's not cheating, it's research! If it's not clear whether a player's proposed solution fits the category, the other players should vote.

Once a solution has been used, it cannot be used again by any player for any puzzle cache during the current game. Once you've solved the puzzle, treat it just like a regular cache. You still need to make a successful Search roll in order to find it. If you fail to find it with your first Search roll, that's okay. You don't need to solve the puzzle again; just continue to search as you would for any other cache.

HYBRID CACHES

It is possible to have caches that are more than one type. For example, the infamous Blood and Guts cache is a 5-stage multi-cache, and also a puzzle cache. These caches are treated as both types for events that affect caches of a certain type.

EQUIPMENT

Various types of geocaching gear are represented in the game using the deck of Equipment cards. Although you don't need any special equipment to geocache (other than a GPS), some of it can be quite helpful. Sometimes the equipment will have a built-in bonus, such as the Flashlight, which helps you when searching.

Other Equipment cards can prevent the effects of certain events. For example, the Insect Repellent can prevent the Mosquitoes or Ticks from bothering you. Most of the time you just need to have the Equipment card on your Tracking Panel to benefit from it. Sometimes, you must discard the card in order to use it. For example, the First-aid Kit can be discarded to prevent most injury events in the game.

You may have up to 4 Equipment cards (played face up on your Tracking Panel). If you already have all the Equipment cards that you are allowed when you draw a new one, you must either discard it or one of your other Equipment cards. Some Equipment cards (such as the Backpack) allow you to have more than 4 Equipment cards in play.

Unlike Event cards, when an Equipment card is discarded, shuffle it back into the deck.

Equipment Combos

At the bottom of each Equipment card, there is a Combo name. For example, the Binoculars and Field Guide cards comprise the Bird Watcher Combo. Each Combo is made up of two Equipment cards. If you're lucky enough to get both cards in a particular Combo, you get a special bonus (shown below).

Some of these special effects (such as the Bird Watcher Combo) are triggered when a particular roll is made. Only rolls that count have a chance of triggering Combo bonuses. When a game effect (such as Determined Dan) allows a player to re-roll the dice, the first roll is ignored. However if a player takes another turn, each of those rolls count, and therefore each roll has a chance of triggering a Combo bonus. Similarly, when a player makes multiple rolls while searching for a Multi-cache, each of those rolls has a chance to trigger events or combo effects.

EVENTS

Whenever you roll to Move or Search, you roll the two 6-sided dice. If you roll doubles (the same number on both dice), you draw an Event card (before moving or searching). The colors on the dice determine when the event takes place. If the colors match (both blue or both yellow), then you may keep that Event card, and play it on yourself or another player later in the game (not on this turn). If the colors differ, then you must reveal the Event card (unless the card says otherwise), and follow the instructions on it.

You may hold up to 3 Event cards. If you are already holding 3 Event cards, you may keep the new one if you like, but you must discard one of your old ones. Keep these cards face-down, so the other players don't know what events you have.

You may play an Event card on any player (including yourself) at the beginning of that player's turn (before the dice are rolled). Only 1 Event card may be played per turn. The current player always gets the chance to play an Event card on himself (if he has one), before another player may do so. If more than one player wants to play an Event card, the player whose turn comes next gets to play it. If the current player rolls doubles after someone has already played an Event card this turn, he keeps the new Event card (whether or not the dice colors matched).

Unlike Equipment cards, when an Event card is played, remove it from the game (unless the card says otherwise). Most events are resolved when they are played, and only affect the current player. In some cases, an event may affect more that one player, or may last one or more rounds. See the Clarifications section for how to handle these events.

New Cache Event

If you roll both Cache icons (the FIND and DNF sides), you have triggered a New Cache Event. Your turn is over (you don't move, or find any cache you may have been searching for), but you get to add a new cache onto the board, and you earn 2 points! Randomly select a cache label to place. See the rules below for how to select a board location. If the cache is not a Micro cache, you also randomly select a Travel Bug label to place in the cache.

No more than 12 caches may be on the board at one time. If you roll the New Cache Event when all 12 cache slots on the Active Caches Panel are full, you don't get to place a new one (and you don't get the 2 bonus points). Instead, you draw a new Travel Bug, and place it on any of the caches which don't already contain one. You get 1 bonus point for placing the Travel Bug. Note that the Hide a Cache Event card is different; it allows you to remove a cache if there are already 12 on the board.

Random Cache Placement

During the game, you may get to add additional caches onto the board. These should be placed randomly. You can choose a random location by rolling the Alphabet die twice (once to determine the latitude/row, and once to determine the longitude/column). The rows and columns are marked with the letters of the alphabet. For example, if you roll "S" and "V", place the cache token in row S, column V. Two caches cannot be on the same tile; roll again if necessary to avoid that.

The Alphabet die has all 26 letters of the alphabet, as well as 4 wilds. If you roll one of the wilds, you can choose any letter you like. If you roll a wild for your column (for example), you may choose any tile along the row you rolled. If you roll wild on your first roll, you may roll for the column before choosing which row you want.

TRAVEL BUGS

Travel Bugs (TBs) are represented in GPS Quest using small labels. Players can acquire TBs by finding a cache that contains them. If the cache you found contains any TBs, you may take one. If you do, move the TB label from the Active Caches Panel onto your Tracking Panel. You may only take one TB, and only if you don't already have two.

If you have any TBs with you when you end your turn on a tile that contains a cache that you have found, you may leave them in that cache. To do so, move the TB label from your Tracking Panel onto the proper cache slot of the Active Caches Panel. You can leave any number of TBs in the cache; you gain 1 point for each one you leave. You can't leave a TB in the same cache that you got it from, or in any Micro cache.

Each TB has a goal (shown on the TB label). If you help a TB reach its goal, then you score 3 points (instead of the 1 point you normally get). For example, you can take the Swimmer TB (shown on the right) to any cache which is next to a Stream to achieve its goal. You may want to re-visit a cache which you have previously found in order to leave a TB. That's fine - you don't have to search for the cache again once you've found it; just end your turn on the tile containing that cache.

Sometimes a TB might start out in a cache that would satisfy its goal; if that happens, you must find another cache in order to earn the 3 points. When a TB reaches its goal, it is removed from the game. In some cases, it may not be possible for a TB to reach its goal; but you can still score 1 point by leaving it in a cache.

CLARIFICATIONS

Caches

  • Project APE: This cache is big. Really big. This makes it pretty easy to find, and it also holds a lot of trinkets. So, when you find this cache, you get to draw four Equipment cards to choose from, even if you're not the FTF. (You still only get to keep one though!)
  • North, To Alaska!: This is a moving cache. After you find this cache, you get to move it. See the Random Cache Placement section for instructions on how to determine a new location.

Cache Characters

  • Jolly Jamie, Kindly Kathy: The Geocoin Equipment cards do count towards a player's total points, but the CITO Bag does not.
  • Independent Isabel: A map quadrant is the separate board piece that represents a quarter of the total map area.
  • Puzzler Paul: Paul may roll for a new letter at the end of his turn, if his token is at the cache. On his next turn, he may use that letter to solve the puzzle instead of one of the regular puzzle letters. He may roll for a new letter once each turn, but he must use at least one of the regular puzzle letters in his solution.

Equipment and Combos

  • Cell Phone: You may choose to take the +1 bonus after you roll.
  • First-aid Kit: Injury Event cards include Blisters, Broken Wrist, and Twisted Ankle.
  • Insulation: For example, if someone plays the Litterbug event card on you, you would only lose 1 point instead of 4.
  • Karma: If the event is discarded, other events may still be played on you this turn.
  • Naturalist: Wildlife Event cards include 13-Year Cicadas, Bear Encounter, Fire Ants, Fox Hunt, Ivory-billed Woodpecker, Killer Bees, Mosquitoes, Ticks, and What Kind of Snake Is That?.

Caches and Travel Bugs

  • Grover: Any Forest tile that is not adjacent to another Forest tile counts as a grove.

Events

  • 13-Year Cicadas: This event lasts two rounds. In other words, it affects all players for their next two turns. Place this card on the Active Caches Panel, so all players can see it. Remove it from the game after it has been in effect for two rounds. Although only one Event card may be played per turn, other events may be played while a multi-turn event is in effect. Other Event cards that last a round or more include: Drought, Equipment Rental, Fresh Snow, and Solar Flares.
  • Bug Swap: If no one else has a Travel Bug, ignore this event.
  • Cache Pirates: Any Travel Bug in the cache is removed from the game.
  • Equipment Rental: If you lose the equipment you rented, you lose 5 points. Renting the Duct Tape to prevent an event would not be a good idea, since you can't return it once you've discarded it. Rented equipment cannot be borrowed or traded.
  • Eureka!: If you're having trouble solving a particular puzzle cache, you may play this card instead. Or you can play it to find a hard cache. It only replaces as a single Search roll though, so you would still have to roll for the other stages of a Multi-cache. Like all Event cards, you must play this card before you roll.
  • Meet and Greet: This is known as an Event Cache in geocaching. Roll the Alphabet die twice to determine the location of the party.
  • Lost and Found: If the player you give the card to doesn't have room, that player must discard a card (they may discard the one you gave them).
  • May I Borrow That?: You can only borrow a pack Equipment card (such as the Backpack, Belt Pack, Hydration Pack, Photographer's Vest, Swag Bag or Waterproof Jacket) if doing so would not cause that player to have more Equipment cards than he's allowed to hold. You can't borrow rented equipment.
  • Hide a Cache: This card works just like rolling the New Cache Event, with one important difference. If there are already 12 caches on the board, this card allows you to remove any cache that you have found -- even if other cachers have not found it yet. However, you may not remove a cache in the same tile as another player's token.
  • Shortcut: Allows you to move up to 6 tiles even if you cross a stream.
  • Upgrade: If there is more than one player with the fewest Equipment cards, you may choose the player. See the Lost and Found clarification above.
  • Weather Forecast: Weather Event cards (also used by the WeathermanCombo) include Bad Weather, Caught in the Rain, Cold Snap, Drought, Early Frost, Flash Flood, Fresh Snow, and It's a Dry Heat.
