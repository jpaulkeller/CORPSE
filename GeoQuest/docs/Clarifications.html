GeoQuest FAQ & Clarifications

Unless the card says otherwise... Some cards will contradict the basic game rules. When that happens, follow the instructions on the card.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What if an event causes me to lose points, but I don't have enough?
A: You can never go below zero.

Cachers

  • Independent Isabel: A map quadrant is a semi-detached board part that represents a quarter of the total map area.
  • Jolly Jamie: The Geocoin does count towards a player's total points, but the CITO Bag does not.

Puzzle -- with examples using and

  • Black or White: Something that is either black or white (or both), such as licorice.
  • First & Last: Any word with the puzzle letters as the first and last letters in the word (either order), such as car.
  • Inside Word: Any word with the puzzle letters inside the word (either order), such as church.
  • Opposites: Two words that are opposites, with one puzzle letter in each word, such as poor and rich.
  • Side by Side: Any word with the puzzle letters next to each other, such as crab.

Equipment and Ensembles

  • Cell Phone: You may decide whether or not to take the +1 bonus after you roll.
  • Emergency Radio, Weatherman: Weather Event cards include Bad Weather, Caught in the Rain, Winter is Coming, Drought, Flash Flood, Fresh Snow, and Heavy Rain.
  • First-aid Kit: Injury Event cards include Blisters, Broken Wrist, and Twisted Ankle.

  • Event Coordinator: If the event is discarded, other events may still be played on you this turn.
  • Naturalist: Wildlife Event cards include Angry Bees, Bear Encounter, Fire Ants, Fox Hunt, Is That Venomous?, Mosquitoes, Rare Bird, Ticks, and Yellow Jackets.
  • Night Cacher: For example, if the Litterbug event card is played on you, you would only lose 1 point instead of 4.

Events

  • All About the Numbers: Only applies to normal caches (not multi or puzzles).
  • Drought: This event affects all players for their next three turns. Other Event cards that last a round or more include: Fresh Snow, Heavy Rain, and Solar Flares. Souvenir Day.
  • Bug Swap: If no one else has a Travel Bug, you still get one and gain 1 point.
  • Caught in the Rain, Landslide: If discarding a pack card would cause you to have from having enough slots for your other equipment, you may (randomly) select a different card to discard.
  • Equipment Rental: If you lose the equipment you rented for any reason, return it to the deck.
  • Eureka!: If you're having trouble solving a particular Puzzle cache, you may play this card instead. Or you can play it to find a difficult cache. It only replaces as a single Search roll though, so you would still have to roll for the other stages of a Multi-cache. Since this is a Play Any Time card, you may play it after you roll.
  • Lost and Found, Upgrade: If the player you give the card to doesn't have a free slot, he must discard a card (he may discard the one you gave him).
  • Meet and Greet, Mega Event: This is known as an Event Cache in geocaching. Draw two Cacher cards to determine the location. Meet and Greet is optional, but all players must attend Mega Event
  • May I Borrow That?: You can only borrow a pack Equipment card (such as the Backpack, Belt Pack, Camel Pack, Jeep, Long Pants, Rain Jacket, Safari Vest, or Swag Bag) if doing so would not cause that player to have more Equipment cards than he's allowed to hold. You can't borrow rented equipment.
  • Shortcut: Allows you to move up to 5 tiles even if you cross a stream.
