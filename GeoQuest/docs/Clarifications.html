CLARIFICATIONS

Unless the card says otherwise... Some cards will contradict the basic game rules. When that happens, follow the instructions on the card.

Cache Characters

  • Jolly Jamie: The Geocoin Equipment cards do count towards a player's total points, but the CITO Bag does not.
  • Independent Isabel: A map quadrant is a semi-detached board part that represents a quarter of the total map area.

Puzzle Cache Categories

  • 5-LETTER WORDS: The solution must be a five letter word.
  • ___ & ___ PHRASES: The solution must be a common phrase such as Salt & Pepper (don't count the word "and").
  • BLACK or WHITE: The solution must be something that is either black or white (or both).
  • FIRST & LAST: The solution can be any word, with the puzzle letters as the first and last letters in the word (either order).
  • SIDE BY SIDE: The solution can be any word, but the puzzle letters must be next to each other.

Equipment and Ensembles

  • Cell Phone: You may choose to take the +1 bonus after you roll.
  • Event Coordinator: If the event is discarded, other events may still be played on you this turn.
  • First-aid Kit: Injury Event cards include Blisters, Broken Wrist, and Twisted Ankle.
  • Naturalist: Wildlife Event cards include Angry Bees, Bear Encounter, Fire Ants, Fox Hunt, Is That Venomous?, Mosquitoes, Rare Bird, Ticks, and Yellow Jackets.
  • Night Cacher: For example, if the Litterbug event card is played on you, you would only lose 1 point instead of 4.
  • Weatherman: Weather Event cards include Bad Weather, Caught in the Rain, Winter is Coming, Drought, Flash Flood, Fresh Snow, and Heavy Rain.

Events

  • Drought: This event lasts two rounds. In other words, it affects all players for their next two turns. Place this card on the map board, so all players can see it. Remove it from the game after it has been in effect for two rounds. Although only one Event card may be played per turn, other events may be played while a multi-turn event is in effect. Other Event cards that last a round or more include: Fresh Snow, Heavy Rain, and Solar Flares. Souvenir Day.
  • Bug Swap: If no one else has a Travel Bug, ignore this event.
  • Cache Pirates: Any Travel Bug in the cache is removed from the game.
  • Equipment Rental: If you lose the equipment you rented for any reason, return it to the deck.
  • Eureka!: If you're having trouble solving a particular Puzzle cache, you may play this card instead. Or you can play it to find a hard cache. It only replaces as a single Search roll though, so you would still have to roll for the other stages of a Multi-cache. Since this is a Discard To Resolve card, you may play it after you roll.
  • Meet and Greet: This is known as an Event Cache in geocaching. Roll the Alphabet die twice to determine the location of the party.
  • Lost and Found: If the player you give the card to doesn't have room, that player must discard a card (they may discard the one you gave them).
  • May I Borrow That?: You can only borrow a pack Equipment card (such as the Backpack, Belt Pack, Camel Pack, Safari Vest, Swag Bag or Rain Jacket) if doing so would not cause that player to have more Equipment cards than he's allowed to hold. You can't borrow rented equipment.
  • Shortcut: Allows you to move up to 6 tiles even if you cross a stream.
  • Upgrade: If there is more than one player with the fewest Equipment cards, you may choose the player. See the Lost and Found clarification above.
